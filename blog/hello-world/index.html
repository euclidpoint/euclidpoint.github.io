Hello, World!

By Euclid Point on 18 Mar 2020

Welcome to Euclid Point.

This community was formed to promote discussion and collaboration between people who love mathematics and computer science. Related areas like algorithms, cryptography, physics, logic puzzles, etc. are on-topic in this community. If you find these topics interesting, you are welcome to join us and be a part of our community. You are also welcome to invite your friends and colleagues and help this community grow.

The name Euclid Point is a tribute to Euclid of Alexandria, the famous Greek mathematician who lived around 300 BCE. Not much is known about Euclid's life but during his lifetime, Euclid compiled ideas in geometry and number theory known to Greek mathematicians at that time into a mathematical treatise consisting of 13 books known as the Elements. It contains a collection of definitions, postulates, propositions, and mathematical proofs. The name Euclid Point is a tribute to the Elements too. This classic treatise in geometry and number theory begins with this simple and profound statement:

A point is that which has no part.

— Euclid's Elements, Book 1, Definition 1

The Elements is considered a masterpiece in the application of logic to mathematics. Euclid's axiomatic approach has had enormous influential in many areas of science, technology, and mathematics over the centuries that have passed sinced then. Euclid's algorithm to find the greatest common divisor (GCD) of two integers is the oldest nontrivial algorithm that has survived to the present day. Euclid's theorem that asserts the infinitude of prime numbers is fundamental to number theory. Therefore, it seemed apt to name this community after the work of a mathematician that has been integral in the development of science and technology in this world.

In this community, we have the following goals in mind:

  1. Be a platform where students and professionals of all backgrounds can come together to learn and discuss mathematics and computer science.
  2. Welcome discussion on related areas such as algorithms, cryptography, physics, etc. as well as related software tools such as \( \LaTeX, \) NumPy, etc.
  3. Discuss mathematical puzzles for fun!

We will be posting regular updates about the activities of our community on our Twitter profile, so please follow us there if you would would like to receive our updates.
